Our Story

Why we built FilterTube — and why it matters

Why FilterTube Exists: The Need

Decluttering for Sanity

FilterTube was created out of a personal necessity and a broader social demand. Platforms like YouTube™ are designed to maximize engagement, not well-being. For individuals with ADHD, neurodivergence, or simply anyone seeking more control over their digital environment, the infinite scroll of unwanted thumbnails, irrelevant recommendations, and noisy suggestions is more than a nuisance — it's a cognitive burden.

This isn't about blocking for the sake of censorship — it's about empowerment. The power to decide what your digital environment looks like. Whether you're a parent, a student, a professional, or someone neurodiverse, you deserve tools that respect your attention and align with your values.

ADHD, Focus, and Mental Fatigue

FilterTube's founder personally lives with ADHD, and the genesis of this tool was directly informed by the struggles of focus loss, dopamine-seeking, and visual overload caused by algorithmic content. The internet is not designed for neurodiverse minds — it overwhelms. FilterTube reclaims that space.

This is not hypothetical. Countless hours lost, frustration, cognitive fog, and a lingering sense of regret — these are daily battles. FilterTube is the line in the sand.

Parents and Guardians: Demanding Better Tools

Discussions across the web — from Reddit threads like r/daddit to the Google™ YouTube Kids™ forum — show how deeply parents crave filtering by keyword, channel, and content sentiment. Comments and entire threads, including contributions from concerned parents (and even the founder of this project), have been deleted or locked — showing how these platforms suppress even discussions of the problem.

The Locked Thread: A Case Study

In 2020, a concerned parent asked a simple question in Google's™ support forum: "How to block videos by keyword or tag?" The thread quickly gained traction as other parents voiced similar frustrations.

What happened next is telling:

  • Google™ refused to implement keyword-based blocking in YouTube Kids™
  • Many parent comments were deleted
  • The thread was locked and remains unresolved years later

See the locked thread here

When moderation tools fail, or are deliberately limited, parents are left exposed. FilterTube fills the void.

Legal Position & Future Vision

Staying Legal

  • While "FilterTube" signals its purpose, we avoid direct usage of YouTube™ trademarks in a way that implies endorsement or affiliation
  • Our extension does not modify or redistribute YouTube's™ code — it operates solely on client-side DOM manipulation through standard browser extension APIs
  • FilterTube does not circumvent any paywalls, DRM, or protected services. It merely hides unwanted elements from the user's view, which is legally comparable to running an ad blocker or custom stylesheet

"Client-side filtering done with user consent and without circumventing access control is a recognized form of personalization and falls under fair use in most jurisdictions."

Minimal Design, Maximum Control

  • FilterTube will always prioritize minimalism — no bloat, no push notifications, no engagement manipulation. Just filtering, fast

Accessibility and Neuro-Inclusion

  • We aim for a UI that respects diverse cognitive styles and screen reader compatibility
  • FilterTube is designed to reduce visual overload — benefiting not just neurodivergent users, but anyone seeking calm

Summary

FilterTube gives you power over your feed without taking power over your data.
