Privacy Policy

Your data stays with you. Always.

Last Updated: May 2025

Welcome to FilterTube! This Privacy Policy explains our approach to data handling and privacy. The short version: we believe privacy isn't a feature—it's a default.

Our Privacy Commitment

FilterTube is built from the ground up with local-first, privacy-respecting design principles. We designed this extension with a clear philosophy: your data should stay with you.

No Cloud, No Collection, No Tracking — Ever

  • FilterTube does not collect or transmit any personal data
  • All filtering operations happen locally in your browser. No user behavior is tracked
  • There are no remote analytics, no telemetry, no hidden calls — zero phone-home behavior

Local Configuration

  • Your keyword lists, blocked channels, and preferences are stored only on your device using standard browser storage (chrome.storage.local)
  • You can edit or delete your filters at any time through the extension popup

Third-Party Services

  • FilterTube does not rely on or embed any third-party analytics, ad networks, or tracking pixels
  • Our open-source ethos ensures transparency; the code is public and auditable

We cannot share, sell, or misuse your data because we don't collect any. It's that simple.

How FilterTube Works

To perform filtering, FilterTube needs to interact with YouTube™ pages you visit. Here's exactly how it works:

Content Processing

  • When you visit YouTube™, our content script runs to analyze the page structure (DOM)
  • The extension compares visible content against your locally stored filters
  • Content matching your filters is hidden using CSS rules
  • This processing happens entirely within your browser — no data leaves your device

Required Permissions

  • Access to youtube.com pages: Needed for our content script to identify and hide elements based on your filters
  • Storage access: Required to save your filter keywords and channel lists locally on your device

These permissions are used exclusively for delivering the core filtering capabilities.

Privacy FAQs

Does FilterTube collect any usage data?

No. FilterTube processes all content locally and does not collect, record, or transmit your browsing activity, filter selections, or any other usage data.

Where are my filters stored?

Your filters (keywords and channel names) are stored exclusively in your browser's local storage using the standard chrome.storage.local API. This data remains on your device and does not sync to any server.

Can Google or YouTube™ see my FilterTube settings?

No. Your FilterTube settings are stored locally and are not accessible to Google, YouTube™, or any third parties. Browser extensions operate in their own isolated environment.

Does FilterTube use any analytics or tracking?

Absolutely not. FilterTube contains no analytics, tracking code, or telemetry of any kind. We deliberately chose not to implement such features, even anonymous usage statistics.

How can I verify FilterTube's privacy claims?

FilterTube is open source, so you (or anyone) can inspect the code directly on our GitHub repository. Additionally, you can use browser developer tools to monitor network requests while using FilterTube—you'll see it doesn't make any external connections.

What happens if I uninstall FilterTube?

Uninstalling FilterTube will remove all your stored filters from your browser's local storage. Since we don't store any data on servers, there's nothing to delete remotely.
